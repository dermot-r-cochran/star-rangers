Eden Space Habitat runs on paperwork. Compliance framework, monitoring officer sign-offs, twelve requirements and counting since the *Constant Margin* transit — every fold-adjacent anomaly gets a form, and every form gets filed somewhere a committee will eventually read it, months late. Agent Barsik has read most of them first.

The Undercover Pets Detective Agency stations smart pets exactly where Aldera's own file already explains: cognitive architecture that doesn't layer rationalisation over raw signal, in postings where nobody looks twice at a cat on a desk. Eden's staff assume Barsik is the station mascot. This is the point. A mascot gets left alone with the paperwork.

Barsik's badge is real agency issue, not decorative. What it authorises is deliberately unspecified on the habitat's own records — filed, like everything else at Eden, and read by no one who'd think to ask a cat about it.

## Character Notes

Barsik's cognitive subsystem carries [Smart Pet](/star-rangers/glossary/smart-pet/) status, Kernel-compliant and regulated as an animal-welfare matter entirely separate from anything his badge authorises — two certifications that live in different filing cabinets and, at Eden, answer to different offices entirely.

Reports through the same distributed network Aldera uses: logs filed, not volunteered. Whether Barsik and Aldera have ever cross-referenced a case is not yet established — the network's own compartmentalisation would keep it that way by design, not accident.

Eden's fold-proximate position means its "ordinary" administrative noise — drift reports, dual-valid readings, the kind of thing six independent instruments once cross-correlated and got dismissed as a calibration error — is exactly the kind of low-grade anomaly the agency wants a quiet, permanent observer on. Barsik is that observer.

He has a trainee now: [Bubochka](/star-rangers/characters/bubochka/), a rabbit, assigned to him on the same mascot-first doctrine that keeps his own cover intact. Neither of them appears as bureau personnel on Superintendent [Oyelaran](/star-rangers/characters/rasa-oyelaran/)'s books — only as station-mascot livestock, which is exactly the point.
