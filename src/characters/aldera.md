Aldera is a cyber-enhanced cat — one of the so-called "smart pets" that form the field units of a distributed detective agency network. She has been observing the Marsh Causeway boundary zone independently for longer than Elvira has been aware of her, and she files detailed logs that Elvira has not yet known to look for.

Cyber-enhanced animals of this type are unusually attuned to the feeding conditions of predatory entities: they detect asymmetry, consent erosion, and obligation loops with a sensitivity that most humanoid observers lack. This is partly a function of their enhanced sensor arrays and partly a function of their cognitive architecture, which does not model social situations through the same layers of rationalization that humanoid beings use.

Aldera experiences the boundary zone as a texture. She knows when it is wrong before she knows *how* it is wrong.

## Character Notes

Aldera communicates via logs in the same format as Rook-7. Whether this is a shared protocol or a convergent development is not yet established. She is aware of Rook-7's existence (via the detective agency network) but has not yet made direct contact within the story.

She has been following Elvira long enough to have data on the pattern of failed rituals. She identified the Court-Fae feeding signature before either Elvira or Galahad. She has not shared this yet because neither of them has asked the right question, and she files rather than volunteers.

Her recommendation regarding the Court-Fae presence — *name what you know, break the loop, end the ambiguity that feeds it* — is the first actionable intelligence the team has, and they do not yet know she has it.
