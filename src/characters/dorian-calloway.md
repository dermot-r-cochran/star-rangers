Dorian Calloway holds the coordination layer nobody notices until it's missing: five habitats' worth of Commissioners, each running a full civil administration and each nominally equal in standing, need one office that can reconcile their competing priorities before any of it reaches Governor [Petra Voss](/star-rangers/characters/petra-voss/) directly. That office is Calloway's.

The post is administrative rather than operational by design — the same design flaw the [Military Space Command](/star-rangers/lore/military-space-command/) never managed to fix in its own consultative councils, except here it was built in deliberately rather than discovered the hard way. Calloway cannot direct a habitat Commissioner's day-to-day policing decisions any more than the [Star Rangers' command ladder can override a specialist corps' guild standards](/star-rangers/lore/star-rangers-command-hierarchy/) — the comparison has occurred to more than one Compact administrator, usually while filling out the paperwork that makes the limitation explicit.

## Orbital Five-O

Commander [Kai Larsen](/star-rangers/characters/kai-larsen/)'s task force reports to the Governor directly for operational tasking, bypassing the Compact's slower consultative councils entirely — that part of Five-O's charter has never changed. What Calloway's office holds is the administrative half: budget reconciliation, cross-habitat logistics, and the standing liaison contact each of the five habitat Commissioners uses when Five-O needs local cooperation rather than local permission. Larsen has described the arrangement, once, as "the Governor decides where I go, and Calloway makes sure nobody's surprised when I get there."

## Character Notes

Calloway reads every one of the five habitats' Commissioner reports personally rather than delegating the first pass to staff, on the theory that a coordination office which stops reading its own inputs closely enough to catch the small discrepancies has already failed at the one thing it exists to do.

Space Commissioner [Zara Wayland](/star-rangers/characters/zara-wayland/)'s Eden filings are, by Calloway's own private assessment, some of the cleanest paperwork the Compact receives — a fact Calloway has never mentioned to Wayland directly, on the same institutional habit that keeps most of this office's actual opinions out of anything that gets filed.
