Naomi Kestrel keeps Eden's noise legible. Habitat telemetry, dock manifests, comm chatter, ration ledgers — to most people it is background hum. To Kestrel it is a continuous, half-readable sentence, and her job is finishing it before someone gets hurt.

She leads the Eden Civil Investigations Unit's field team by default rather than by title — nobody above her has formally said so, and she has never asked them to. She reads a room the way she reads a data feed: for the one entry that doesn't belong.

Assigned to Eden Space Habitat two years into its expansion, Kestrel arrived as a junior systems auditor and stayed once she realised the habitat's civilian population generated more genuine mysteries than its instrument arrays did. Fold-adjacent research platforms attract brilliant, overworked scientists, restless dependents, and, occasionally, people with something to hide. Kestrel finds the third category first.

## Character Notes

She reports, along with the rest of her team, to a superior neither of them has met in person — a voice on a secure channel known to them only as Control, who assigns cases, withholds context, and is never available to explain why.

Kestrel has stopped asking why. The cases close either way.

## The Unit

Kestrel, [Rosalind Vey](/star-rangers/characters/rosalind-vey/), and [Tamsin Reyes](/star-rangers/characters/tamsin-reyes/) make up the whole of Eden's junior field roster — three investigators for a research platform of several thousand residents, which everyone including Control agrees is not enough, and which none of them have been given the budget to fix.

They manage regardless. Kestrel keeps the data honest, Vey keeps the room secure, and Reyes keeps the room talking. Together they close more cases than the roster size suggests they should.
