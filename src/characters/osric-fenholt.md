Osric Fenholt held one of the least distinguished titles the Imperium's Belt Settlements Directorate had to offer — Assistant Deputy Viceroy, a post two full grades below anyone with actual standing to set policy — and spent nineteen years using it to file the only settlement condition reports in the Directorate's archive that said what was actually happening.

He was not a dissident. He never joined the Reform Movement, never made a public claim, and would have rejected "honest" as a description of what he did, on the grounds that it implied a choice. To Fenholt, filing the correct figures was simply the job as written, and he found it faintly insulting that anyone treated doing it as remarkable.

## Known History

- Born 2558 UCSD to a technical family on the Ceres administrative belt; commissioned into the Bureau of Outer Compliance's technical track — the route Imperium doctrine reserved for civilian expertise reframed as support for Imperium operations, since the Bureau's own advancement ladder ran through service record rather than qualification.
- Appointed Assistant Deputy Viceroy for the Anchorpoint Cluster in 2590 UCSD, responsible for compiling quarterly settlement condition reports on eleven Belt habitats and forwarding them through a Deputy Viceroy and Viceroy to Solar Command.
- Refused, across nineteen years and several changes of superior, to alter the figures underneath his reports to match the more optimistic language his reviewing officers routinely layered on top of them — most notably in the 2601 Anchorpoint Station supply audit, where a documented 34% shortfall reached his desk annotated "describe as manageable." Fenholt left the number untouched and appended a note observing that "manageable" did not appear as a defined category anywhere in Directorate Schedule 4, and asking the reviewing officer to specify which schedule category applied, or else let the figure stand as submitted.
- Survived where a more visible dissenter would not have. The Imperium's own doctrine of technical rigor and its intolerance of ambiguity gave his accuracy nowhere to be filed as sedition — a correctly categorized number read, to the institution that demanded correct categories, as competence rather than criticism.
- Remained in post through the Imperium's final decade and its 2609 UCSD restructuring into the Military Space Command; declined a continuing appointment under the new command structure and retired in 2611 UCSD.
- Died 2621 UCSD. His nineteen years of unaltered raw reports — routinely resummarized by officers above him but never once amended in his own hand — became, once historians and the Reform Movement's successors went looking for a Belt settlement record the Imperium hadn't had the chance to edit, one of the few genuinely contemporaneous documentary sources for the period.

## Character Notes

Colleagues who worked beneath Fenholt describe his method as tedious rather than brave: he asked, in writing, exactly which defined category a euphemism belonged to, and left the underlying number exactly where it was until someone answered. Most reviewing officers eventually stopped trying to correct him, less out of principle than arithmetic — arguing with a footnote cost more paperwork than filing the figure as submitted, and the Directorate's own procedures gave him every right to ask the question.

He kept no copies of his own reports beyond what the Bureau's retention schedule required, on the stated grounds that a compliance officer who hoarded records "was describing himself, not the settlements." The habitats under his cluster fared no better and no worse, materially, than any other under Imperium administration. What changed, quietly, was that anyone who later wanted to know how they had actually fared had somewhere reliable to look.
