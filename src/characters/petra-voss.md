Petra Voss presides over five self-governing orbital habitats around Earth, bound together under the [Orbital Habitats Compact](/star-rangers/lore/orbital-habitats-compact/) — a civil jurisdiction that has nothing to do with, and does not overlap, the Star Rangers' charter-limited authority. [Rangers have no authority inside self-governing orbital habitats](/star-rangers/lore/military-space-command/) at all; Voss's office is the government that governs where the Rangers, by charter, cannot.

For two years before she commissioned [Orbital Five-O](/star-rangers/characters/kai-larsen/), Voss argued the same problem from the administrative side that [Kai Larsen](/star-rangers/characters/kai-larsen/) had argued from the Ranger side before resigning over it: her five habitats had jurisdiction and no investigative capacity worth the name. She solved it the way she solves most structural gaps in the Compact — not by restructuring the whole apparatus, but by commissioning one office with narrow authority and a direct line to her, and letting it bypass everything slower.

## Structure Beneath Her

Chief Commissioner [Dorian Calloway](/star-rangers/characters/dorian-calloway/) handles the Compact's day-to-day coordination across all five habitats. Each habitat runs its own Commissioner — [Zara Wayland](/star-rangers/characters/zara-wayland/) at [Eden](/star-rangers/lore/eden-space-habitat/) — answering upward to Calloway and, in parallel, to that habitat's own elected Mayor. Voss designed the dual line deliberately, on the reasoning that a Compact office accountable only upward eventually stops being accountable to the residents it governs at all.

## Character Notes

Voss commissioned Larsen specifically because he had resigned his Ranger commission over a jurisdictional wall rather than learning to work around it — she wanted an investigator who would treat the Compact's own jurisdiction limits with the same seriousness he'd once demanded of the Rangers', not one who would quietly expand Five-O's mandate the first time it proved convenient. So far, he has.

She reads Five-O's case reports personally rather than through Calloway's office, the one deliberate exception to her own coordination-layer design — on the theory that an investigative unit answering to her directly should also be read by her directly, or the "directly" stops meaning anything.
