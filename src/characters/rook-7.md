Rook-7 is a bipedal robot on an older civilian chassis, but its sensor arrays are non-standard and its civilian classification appears to be either outdated or deliberately understated. It logs continuously. It flags anomalies. It draws conclusions and labels them as hypotheses until they are confirmed.

It is one of the "smart pets and robots" detective agency units described in the field as excellent anti-beautiful-monster sensors: robots and cyber-enhanced animals are unusually good at detecting asymmetry, consent erosion, and obligation loops — the feeding conditions of Court-Fae, Gilded Saints, and other predatory entities.

## Character Notes

Rook-7 communicates primarily through logs. Its logs are not objective: they contain assessments, hypotheses, recommendations, and occasional dry observations that function as commentary. Whether Rook-7 is aware of the commentary dimension of its logs is not yet established.

It does not sit down unless there is a specific reason to do so. This is not a protocol; it is a preference.

Its non-standard sensor arrays have not been fully explained. Their origin is flagged as a follow-up item in its own logs.

## Role in the Team

Rook-7 fills the role of the detective: it notices what others explain away, names patterns before others are ready to name them, and accumulates observations without demanding resolution before the data supports it. It is not impatient. It is thorough.

Its greatest current limitation is that it can identify the conditions for boundary events and predatory-entity feeding, but it cannot yet predict timing.
