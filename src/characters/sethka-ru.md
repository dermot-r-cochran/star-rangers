Kharis Belt IV has thin atmosphere, high UV flux, and a thermal inversion between day and night that would kill an unprotected person in under an hour on either side. Sethka Ru was born there. He learned to read margins before he learned to read routes.

He is Serephine Dunekin. The physiology is built for sparse-signal environments: light-scattering eye membranes, water-conservative metabolism, respiratory efficiency that makes dense-atmosphere work manageable but never easy. In station atmosphere he wears filtration gear mostly for comfort, not survival.

He is at his best when visibility is long, interference is low, and a mission depends on positional discipline more than speed.

## Character Notes

Sethka plans movement around exposure windows. He times routes by radiation profile, glare angle, and thermal shadow. Then he checks whether the social plan can survive the physical reality. It does not always.

Because his homeworld punished small navigation errors with lethal consequences, he is precise about margins and skeptical of mission briefs that assume stable conditions without measuring them first. *Assume stable* is not a methodology. It is a wish.
