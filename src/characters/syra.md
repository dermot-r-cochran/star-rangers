Syra is Krenyi — one of the Quiet-Built, a long-lived people who are physically attractive by most humanoid standards, maintain a high female birthrate, and cannot interbreed with humans or any other species. She is approximately one hundred and fifty years old, which is not particularly old for her kind.

She does not experience deep fear, though she experiences anxiety. She does not experience fury, though she experiences annoyance. She is polyamorous but loyal. She is honest to a degree that some humans find uncomfortable and others find clarifying.

She arrived at Threshold Station in 2826 CE having read the public boundary survey data and identified the chronometer discrepancy as something the public boundary survey data had incorrectly classified.

## Character Notes

Syra resists ambiguity not because she dislikes uncertainty, but because ambiguity is a feeding condition for certain kinds of entities that she has encountered before. She keeps her models explicit and her assessments clear. When she does not know something, she says so.

She is initially trusting of new contacts and can be briefly taken in by human social deceptions she has not previously encountered. She detects pattern drift — the moment when a social situation stops matching its stated terms — and withdraws relevance from it. She does not make scenes. She simply stops participating.

She will not be mythologised. If someone begins to treat her as a symbol, an omen, or an archetype, she names it and returns the conversation to material terms.

## On the Krenyi term

"Krenyi" is used in story to avoid confusion with Naiads (river-bound entities, not persons). "Nai" as a state means "still relevant here" — it is not a greeting or a title but an acknowledgement that she remains present and engaged.
