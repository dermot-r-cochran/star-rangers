Tamsin Reyes can become whoever a room needs her to be for exactly as long as the job requires it, and not one conversation longer. Cover identity, accent, posture, the particular kind of forgettable that keeps people talking past the point they should have stopped — all of it is craft, not instinct, and she has spent years sharpening it.

She works Eden's civilian population the way [Naomi Kestrel](/star-rangers/characters/naomi-kestrel/) works its telemetry: patiently, for the one detail that doesn't belong. Where Kestrel finds the anomaly in a data feed, Reyes finds it in what somebody's spouse doesn't quite say at a dinner two decks over.

She dislikes admitting how much of her success depends on people wanting, very badly, to be listened to. It works too well and too consistently for her to keep pretending otherwise.

## Character Notes

Reyes keeps no written record of her cover identities beyond what Control requires for the case file, on the theory that a good cover should not survive being looked for. [Rosalind Vey](/star-rangers/characters/rosalind-vey/) finds this alarming. Reyes finds Vey's alarm reassuring, on the theory that someone should be keeping track even if it isn't her.

She is, by a wide margin, the team's best interviewer and its worst correspondent. Case reports filed under her name arrive late, are technically complete, and read like they were dictated at speed to someone who found the whole exercise faintly beneath her.
