*Archivist's note (S., Survey Archive): I am filing this under reconciliation, not music, and I want the reason on the record. A ballad that keeps a Line Captain's story open and beautiful and never quite closed is not, by itself, a harm — grief has always sung. But it is the exact shape the [counter-doctrine](/star-rangers/lore/predatory-entities/) tells us to watch: a sanctified, unfinished sacrifice that resolution never quite reaches, sung by people who have strong and good reasons to keep singing it. Whether anything is feeding on that, or whether it is only us, is [the open question of the whole file](/star-rangers/codex/the-warm-edge-correlation/). So I keep the song, and I keep the caution beside it, and I decline — the way she would have — to decide which one it is. She was shown something. We were shown a little of it, secondhand, at a warm edge. That is not the same as knowing how it ends, and a song that sounds like it knows should be trusted exactly as far as that.*
