**RECOVERED RECORDING — AUDIO + TRANSCRIPTION**
**Performing credit:** Slipwave
**Recorded:** Eden Space Habitat, same season as the [Star Rangers Anthem](/star-rangers/codex/star-rangers-anthem/) recording
**Current standing:** Circulates less widely than the anthem. Not sung at musters — this one gets played, not performed, usually by whoever is waiting on a ship that hasn't docked yet.

*Transcriber's note: unlike the anthem, this recording survives as one continuous dock-circuit copy rather than several partial ones. The lyric sheet below matches it line for line.*

---

> Verse 1
>
> Dock lights count the hours you're gone,
> the manifest says three years, maybe four.
> No signal outruns the hull that carries it,
> so I'm writing this letter to your door.
>
> Chorus
>
> Sing me a ballad of the stars,
> sing it slow, sing it low, sing it true.
> Every light up there was somebody's someone,
> carrying a name the long way home to you.
>
> Verse 2
>
> They say the fold don't care who's waiting,
> says the boundary doesn't keep a clock.
> But I keep mine running on your schedule,
> and I meet every ship that makes the dock.
>
> Chorus
>
> Sing me a ballad of the stars,
> sing it slow, sing it low, sing it true.
> Every light up there was somebody's someone,
> carrying a name the long way home to you.
>
> Verse 3
>
> Some folks say the causeway swallows people,
> some folks say it just slows down the years.
> I don't need the archive to explain it —
> I just need you standing here.
>
> Bridge
>
> If the record disagrees on what happened,
> let it argue. I was there. I know.
> I don't need the truth to be official,
> I just need you coming home.
>
> Chorus (out)
>
> Sing me a ballad of the stars,
> sing it slow, sing it low, sing it true.
> Every light up there was somebody's someone,
> carrying a name the long way home,
> carrying a name the long way home to you.
