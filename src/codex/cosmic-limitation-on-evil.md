**DOCTRINAL WORKING PAPER — REVISED FILING**
**Prepared by:** Theorist Emrys Kade, Doctrinal Division
**Subject:** The Cosmic Limitation on Evil, and its standing against the Cascade's Global Invariants
**Status:** Contested; unresolved after multiple revision cycles

---

Ask an Archive theorist whether the Cascade punishes evil and, for decades, the answer was the same: no, that's a category error. The Cosmic Limitation on Evil is the one confirmed exception, and this division has never been fully comfortable with it. This paper exists because every attempt to retire that discomfort by rephrasing the law has failed, and the failures are now part of the doctrinal record in their own right.

The **Cosmic Limitation on Evil** is a law attributed to [Kieme](/star-rangers/glossary/kieme/): every entity that exists in service of evil will dissolve and fade once it no longer serves any higher purpose. Unlike Kieme's other nonlocal constraints, this division's own filings do not describe it as merely inferred from an absence of counterexamples — the working consensus, such as it is, describes it as *designed*: a deliberate act of the Upper Structure rather than a passive boundary condition. That consensus is itself the source of everything below.

## The Constraint, as Filed

No entity, institution, pattern, or persistent effect that exists in service of evil can outlast the higher purpose it was bent toward. Once that purpose is exhausted — once the harm stops serving whatever end justified the Cascade tolerating it at all — the entity dissolves and fades. Predatory and parasitic beings that feed on suffering are the clearest documented case; see [Predatory Entities](/star-rangers/lore/predatory-entities/) for the classification this division cross-checks against. This paper does not commit to when or how that exhaustion occurs in any specific case — see the closing note.

The dissolution is not swift, and it is not merciful. It is owed.

## Cosmic Debt

All evil and all suffering, per this division's working doctrine, creates a **cosmic debt** — an imbalance the universe carries until it is repaid. Kieme is credited with repaying it, on whatever schedule and by whatever mechanism Kieme alone determines. No confirmed instance of a cosmic debt going permanently unpaid has ever been documented — though this division notes that "no confirmed instance" and "no instance" are not the same claim, and has never closed the file on the distinction.

Neither the timeline nor the mechanism of repayment is specified anywhere in the record this division holds. That silence is treated, provisionally, as doctrine rather than evasion: a debt whose repayment could be predicted would stop functioning as a warning to whoever is currently running one up. This division records that reasoning without fully endorsing it.

## The Exception This Division Keeps Explaining Away

The [Cascade's Global Invariants](/star-rangers/lore/cosmic-cascade/) state, flatly, that there is no moral cosmology — no virtue, no sin, no reward, no punishment. The Cosmic Limitation on Evil is the one law that reads, on its face, like exactly that. Successive generations of this division's theorists have tried to rephrase it as structural rather than moral — load-bearing capacity, not judgment — and every rephrasing has struggled to explain why the law only ever targets evil and never anything else. Devotional tradition has never needed the rephrasing. It has simply called the Limitation what it looks like: Kieme keeping the ledger.
