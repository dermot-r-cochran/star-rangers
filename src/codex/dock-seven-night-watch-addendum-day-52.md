### Context

Filed after manager closure note on [Incident Report 001](/star-rangers/codex/incident-report-001/).  
Purpose: capture recurring Dock Seven timing asymmetry for watch continuity, even if no new incident number is opened.

### Observed Pattern

At 01:22 station time, C-7A and C-7B again diverged by forty seconds after a clean reset at shift start.

- C-7A remained exactly +00:40 relative to C-7B for the full watch window.
- No progressive drift was recorded.
- Relay diagnostics remained nominal.
- Environmental monitors in Dock Seven showed no thermal or electromagnetic spike.

### Operational Effect

Routine docking remained possible, but dual-clock confirmation procedures added delay to every vessel handoff.  
Traffic Control flagged the delay as manageable now, but unsafe under surge conditions.

### Note on Classification

Current classification remains "calibration issue."  
Watch assessment: classification is no longer operationally useful. The recurrence profile matches structured anomaly behaviour, not ordinary instrument wear.

### Continuity Recommendation

1. Keep dual-clock verbal callouts active for all night-watch docking transactions.
2. Keep this addendum attached to [First Drift Report Filed at Threshold Station](/star-rangers/timeline/year-minus-11-first-drift-report/) in local briefing packets.
3. Escalate to external survey review if any widening beyond forty seconds is observed.
