**RECITED AT EVERY FIRST LANDING SINCE**
**Compiler unrecorded — the First Assembly's own minutes credit "the whole company, together"**

---

We came expecting the mask and the dome. We packed for it. We grieved for it in advance, the way the Fleet's own charter told us to grieve: for the world we would never walk unaided, for the sky we would only ever see through composite glass.

The hatch opened on Mannán's own shore, and the air came in, and it was air, and we breathed it, and none of us had the training to know what to do with a mercy we had not budgeted for.

Some among the company said at once: this is Manannán's own gift, the sea-god keeping the old promise his name still carries — that the way to Tír na nÓg, once found, would not turn its finder away at the door. Others said only: the survey team will explain it in the morning, and morning came, and the survey team could not.

We do not require agreement on the cause. We require only that the company remember, every year this is recited, that we stepped off the ark owing a debt we have not finished naming, to a shore, a sea, or a mercy we cannot fully account for — and that the honest accounting begins with owning that we do not know which of the three it was.

*So the First Assembly recorded it, and so it has been recited at every First Landing anniversary since, unedited, by request of every Assembly that has since had the authority to edit it.*

---

*Archivist's note, Union Cultural Archive: the "old promise" the text references is Manannán mac Lir's traditional role, in the founding cooperative's inherited Irish mythology, as the guardian who permits or denies passage to the Otherworld — a role the founders' own naming of both the system and the world made explicit before landing, not after. Whether the text's authors intended "Manannán's own gift" literally, as devotional colour, or as a placeholder for an explanation they knew they didn't have, is not resolved by the text itself and has never been resolved by anyone who compiled it. See [Frontier Transformation Protocols](/star-rangers/lore/frontier-transformation-protocols/) and the accompanying [survey note on competing theories](/star-rangers/codex/tir-na-nog-compatibility-survey-note/) for the institutional side of the same open question.*
