**RECOVERED RECORDING — TRANSCRIPTION**
**Original title:** *Half-Light Causeway*
**Performing credit:** Slipwave
**Recorded:** Eden Space Habitat, roughly one year before the performer's cadet intake
**Current standing:** No formal charter status. Sung at cadet musters and informal commissionings across multiple intakes; never adopted by any Star Rangers directive, never disclaimed by one either.

---

*Transcriber's note: no studio master survives. This text is reconstructed from three independently carried copies — a dock recording, a cadet-cohort recording made years later at a muster, and Shepherd's own handwritten lyric sheet, recovered separately. The three agree closely enough that the differences are treated as performance variation, not competing versions.*

---

> Verse 1
>
> Threshold clock is running slow again,
> same eleven years it never learns to close.
> Every dock has a light it can't explain,
> every causeway has a story no one chose.
>
> Chorus
>
> The stars call us forward with hope —
> protect what is good, and learn what is true.
> Half-light causeway, edge of the known,
> I'll carry the lamp if you carry it too.
>
> Verse 2
>
> Nobody signed me up to hold the line,
> nobody handed me a rank to grow into.
> But the boundary doesn't ask for a title first,
> it only asks if the work will get done.
>
> Chorus
>
> The stars call us forward with hope —
> protect what is good, and learn what is true.
> Half-light causeway, edge of the known,
> I'll carry the lamp if you carry it too.
>
> Bridge
>
> Let the record argue whose witness was right.
> Let the archive keep fighting for one true line.
> I'm not here to settle the history books —
> I'm here because the light was still on, and I saw it.
>
> Chorus (out)
>
> The stars call us forward with hope —
> protect what is good, and learn what is true.
> Half-light causeway, edge of the known,
> I'll carry the lamp if you carry it too,
> I'll carry the lamp if you carry it too.
