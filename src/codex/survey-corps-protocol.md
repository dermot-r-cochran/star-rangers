**SURVEY CORPS**
**BOUNDARY MONITORING PROTOCOL**
**CLASS: THRESHOLD STATIONS**
**Issued:** Year -15
**Current version:** 2.3

---

## Section 1 — Purpose

This protocol governs boundary monitoring activities at all stations classified as Threshold-class — that is, stations located within 500 survey-units of a confirmed Concordant boundary.

The purpose of Threshold-class boundary monitoring is to:
1. Maintain baseline measurement of boundary position and stability.
2. Report significant deviations in the boundary's position or behaviour.
3. Support research into Concordant boundary characteristics.

## Section 2 — Instrument Standards

All Threshold-class stations are required to maintain a minimum of two independently calibrated chronometers. Discrepancies between chronometers of greater than one second are to be reported as an instrument anomaly and investigated.

*[Note in margin, handwriting, unsigned: "The two-chronometer requirement was added in Year -17 after an incident at Station Kelvar where a 3-minute discrepancy went undetected for six months. The requirement has never been followed up with any guidance on what to do when the discrepancy does not resolve on recalibration."]*

## Section 3 — Reporting

Anomalous readings are to be filed as Incident Reports within 24 hours of detection. Incident Reports are reviewed by the station manager and forwarded to the regional survey office if the manager classifies them as requiring external review.

Station managers have full authority to classify Incident Reports as resolved without external review.

## Section 4 — Authorised Activities

Survey teams at Threshold-class stations may conduct:
- Physical inspection of boundary-proximate areas
- Instrument reading and logging
- Material sampling in boundary-proximate zones

Survey teams may not conduct:
- Etheric-layer assessment without a certified analyst assigned to the team
- Boundary contact procedures without regional office authorisation

---

*Appendix: Certified Analyst Classifications*

A certified boundary analyst is defined as a being with documented training in Etheric-layer assessment methods and certification by a recognised training body.
