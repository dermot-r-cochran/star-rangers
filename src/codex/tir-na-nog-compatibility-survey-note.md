**JOINT SURVEY NOTE — XENOBIOLOGY OFFICE**
**Subject: Tír na nÓg, biosphere-compatibility origin — status of competing theories**
**Classification: Open Question, Standing Review**

---

Four theories currently circulate for why Tír na nÓg is the only surveyed world in Solar System record naturally compatible with unmodified human atmosphere and gravity. This office has closed none of them. What follows is a fair accounting of all four, including the one this office is asked about most often and trusts least.

**1. Convergent evolution (the founding cooperatives' own working theory, still printed in Union tourist literature).** Tír na nÓg's native biochemistry independently arrived at carbon-based, oxygen-respiring life patterns close enough to Earth's own for cross-compatibility. Plausible in isolation. Statistically strained as the sole confirmed instance against several hundred other surveyed biospheres, none of which converged the same way. This office's working assessment: incomplete, not false.

**2. Undetected precursor engineering.** A civilisation with no other confirmed trace in the system deliberately seeded or adjusted the biosphere before the founding cooperative's arrival. No physical evidence has ever been recovered — no structure, no artifact, no residue any survey instrument has flagged. Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence, but this office notes plainly that "no evidence after four generations of continuous habitation and repeated survey passes" is a weaker position than its proponents usually present it as.

**3. An undetected Concordant-level mechanism.** Some structural feature of Concordant law biases convergence toward Earth-compatible chemistry at this specific site, for reasons not yet modelled. This is, functionally, an admission that current physics cannot explain the result — which this office regards as the most honest of the four theories precisely because it makes no claim beyond what's actually known.

**4. Levrilic influence or divine providence.** By a wide margin, the most popular opinion among the Union's own public, and the position this office is asked to rule out most often by visiting delegations who assume it already has been. It has not.

## On Theory 4 Specifically

The popularity of Theory 4 is not difficult to explain: the founding cooperative's own thanksgiving text, recited at every First Landing anniversary since, frames the compatibility explicitly as a possible gift from Manannán mac Lir rather than a natural accident — see the [First Breath thanksgiving](/star-rangers/codex/first-breath-a-founding-thanksgiving/) for the primary text — and the Union's more recent public familiarity with [Levrils](/star-rangers/lore/levrils/) as documented, Cascade-ranked meta-dimensional beings has given the old devotional framing a modern vocabulary to reattach to. The methodological precedent is real: [Brother Daire's account of Saint Aoife's vision](/star-rangers/codex/life-of-saint-aoife/) is, on the Union's own scholarship, a plausible unrecognised Levril encounter recorded centuries before anyone had the classification to name it. That precedent is why this office takes the question seriously rather than dismissing it outright.

It is also why this office cannot confirm it. A genuine Levril-proximate event should leave "instrument anomalies consistent with extra-dimensional field intrusion," per the Levril record's own documented pattern. No such anomaly has been recovered from any instrument reading taken on or near Tír na nÓg, across four generations of continuous habitation, including several dedicated retrospective sweeps run specifically to look for one. Divine providence, in whatever form the cooperative's own founders meant it, is not a claim this office is equipped to evaluate at all, and declines to try.

## Standing Recommendation

This office does not recommend closing the file on any of the four theories, including the one it trusts least. A single confirmed anomaly against several hundred negative results is not a result popular opinion is obligated to wait politely for an explanation of, and this office has stopped expecting the public position and the institutional one to converge before new data arrives — if it ever does.
