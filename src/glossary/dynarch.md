A **Dynarch** (pronounced: **DY-nark**; plural: **Dynarchs**; from *dynamis*, force) is a Cascade-tier being that encapsulates and governs a physical law or force — **Gravity**, **Electromagnetics**, or **Nature** (biological life), among others. Each Dynarch embodies one force-domain: it is not a mind that operates the force from outside, but the tier at which that force is lawfully administered at all.

Dynarchs rank between [Concordant Principals](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant-principal/) and [Celestials](/star-rangers/glossary/celestials/), and they are **aligned with the [Telearchs](/star-rangers/glossary/telerarch/)** — their function is continuity of forces across zone boundaries and universes, which is Telearch-side work, not resident governance. A Concordant Principal outranks a Dynarch in-universe but cannot command one; the Dynarch's obligations run up the Telearch line.

**Key constraints:**
- Dynarchs govern the *operation* of a force, not its design: the rule-sets are the Worldwright's, and a Dynarch cannot rewrite a zone's physics.
- Alignment with the Telearchs binds them to continuity over local preference — a Dynarch keeps its force coherent across boundaries even where zone rule-sets differ.
- Dynarchs do not perform enforcement actions; rule-integrity intervention remains Celestial work.

**Legacy usage:** older records call this tier **higher celestials** — a term also used loosely for Telearchs (as "High Celestials"). The ambiguity is why both usages were retired; see [High Celestials](/star-rangers/glossary/high-celestials/) for the disambiguation rule.

**Example:** Gravity does not fail in a boundary zone, even where timebases disagree and instruments quarrel — the force stays administered across the seam. That cross-boundary coherence is Dynarch work, and it is also why low-context observers have worshipped this tier more than any other: a being that *is* the storm, the tide, or the living green reads as a god to anyone who cannot see the ladder it stands on.
