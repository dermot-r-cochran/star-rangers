A **Dynarch** (pronounced: **DY-nark**; plural: **Dynarchs**; from *dynamis*, force) is a Cascade-tier being that encapsulates and governs a physical law or force — **Gravity**, **Electromagnetics**, or **Nature** (biological life), among others. Each Dynarch embodies one force-domain: it is not a mind that operates the force from outside, but the tier at which that force is lawfully administered at all.

Dynarchs rank between [Concordant Principals](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant-principal/) (Harmonarchs) and [Celestials](/star-rangers/glossary/celestials/), and they are **co-created** — defined jointly by both branches of the Cascade matrix rather than by a single parent tier. They stand under *both* lines: zone-local operation of a force runs with the zone's Principal, while continuity of that force across zone boundaries and universes runs up the [Telearch](/star-rangers/glossary/telerarch/) line, with which the Dynarchs are aligned. In the Cascade, higher strata create and define the layers beneath them more than they command them — a Dynarch's dual obligation is written into what it *is*, not imposed on it order by order.

**Key constraints:**
- Dynarchs govern the *operation* of a force, not its design: the rule-sets are the Worldwright's, and a Dynarch cannot rewrite a zone's physics.
- Dual obligation to Principal and Telearch; where zone-local preference and cross-boundary continuity conflict, continuity prevails.
- Dynarchs do not perform enforcement actions; rule-integrity intervention remains Celestial work.

**Legacy usage:** older records call this tier **higher celestials** — a term also used loosely for Telearchs (as "High Celestials"). The ambiguity is why both usages were retired; see [High Celestials](/star-rangers/glossary/high-celestials/) for the disambiguation rule.

Dynarchs are also the lowest of the Cascade's timeless strata: every tier above the [Celestials](/star-rangers/glossary/celestials/) is timeless and eternal, and the Dynarchs stand last on the eternal side of that line.

**Example:** Gravity does not fail in a boundary zone, even where timebases disagree and instruments quarrel — the force stays administered across the seam. That cross-boundary coherence is Dynarch work, and it is also why low-context observers have worshipped this tier more than any other: a being that *is* the storm, the tide, or the living green — and that time does not touch — reads as a god to anyone who cannot see the ladder it stands on.
