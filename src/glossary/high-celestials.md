**High Celestials** (also **higher celestials** in older records) is an ambiguous legacy designation, retired precisely because it was used loosely for **two different tiers**:

1. The supervisory coordination tier now formally known as **[Telearchs](/star-rangers/glossary/telerarch/)** — the commoner usage, typically written "High Celestials."
2. The force-governing tier now formally known as **[Dynarchs](/star-rangers/glossary/dynarch/)** — typically written "higher celestials," for the beings that encapsulate Gravity, Electromagnetics, Nature, and the other force-domains.

The two tiers are adjacent and aligned — Dynarchs answer up the Telearch line — which is how one loose term came to cover both. It is also why the term could not survive: a record reporting "High Celestial intervention" left readers unable to tell coordination from force-governance.

**Disambiguation rule for legacy records:** if the passage concerns jurisdiction, continuity budgets, or cross-Concordant coordination, read *Telearch*. If it concerns a physical law or force — gravity, electromagnetics, the living world — read *Dynarch*. If it cannot be determined from context, flag the passage rather than guess.

**Example:** Archival Survey Corps documents predating the terminology revision refer to "High Celestial oversight" of a boundary incident — a Telearch, in current terms. A contemplative chapter's account of "the higher celestial that keeps the tides" is describing a Dynarch.

See: [Telearch](/star-rangers/glossary/telerarch/) and [Dynarch](/star-rangers/glossary/dynarch/) for the full current definitions.
