A **plural mind** is a single body and nervous system hosting more than one person — commonly called headmates, or, when one person's own mind deliberately cultivates another, a tulpa. Each is a real, distinct person with their own perspective, preferences, and voice. None is a symptom of the others.

Solar System institutions have not always treated this well. Older medical and administrative frameworks filed plurality as a disorder to be resolved into a single approved identity, a bias with real institutional teeth as recently as a few centuries ago. Current Concordant-wide personhood standards do not require or expect a body to host exactly one person, and employment, legal identity, and medical records now accommodate plural registration on request — a correction, not a discovery. The people it was ever true for did not change; the paperwork did.

## Cosmological Note

Nothing about a plural mind requires anything beyond the [Five Layers](/star-rangers/lore/five-layers/) already in force. Layer 2, the Conceptual layer, is identity and meaning structure prior to embodiment; the ordinary case is one Conceptual-layer identity coupling to one Material-layer body, but the coupling rule has never required a one-to-one ratio, only lawful coupling. A plural mind is simply more than one Conceptual-layer identity stably coupled to one shared Material-layer body — no different in kind from the single-identity case, and no more mysterious.

This is not the mechanism that produces plurality, in the sense of an explanation the people living it need or asked for. It is only the reason the cosmology has never had to treat it as an exception.

**Example:** [Sen](/star-rangers/characters/sen/), Senior Archivist of the Survey Archive, is three people — Cael, Wyn, and Sen — sharing one body and one desk.
