**Slipwave** was a three-person spacer-folk band that played Eden Space Habitat's civilian arts circuit for a little under two years, several seasons before its vocalist, [Tissadelle Shepherd](/star-rangers/characters/tissadelle-shepherd/), enlisted as a Star Rangers cadet. The name refers to the pitch drift habitat musicians learned to compensate for when a station's rotational hum leaks into an unshielded sound system — a small, specific, entirely civilian problem, and a deliberately unglamorous name for a band that mostly played dock-adjacent common rooms.

Slipwave never recorded a studio album. Their surviving catalogue is a handful of habitat-circuit recordings, most of them incomplete or badly mic'd. One is not.

## The Recording

The exception is the track later adopted, without any formal decision by anyone, as the [Star Rangers Anthem](/star-rangers/codex/star-rangers-anthem/). Shepherd wrote and sang it roughly a year before her cadet intake. It circulated quietly among Eden's dock crews after the band quietly stopped playing together, and it kept circulating after Shepherd left for cadet training — carried, as most things at the edge of the frontier are, by whoever happened to be traveling.

By the time Shepherd made Line Captain, cadet cohorts several intakes removed from her own were already singing it at musters without reliably knowing who had written it. She has never corrected the record in public. She has also never denied it.

## Status

Slipwave did not formally disband. Its two other members remain on Eden's civilian roster; Shepherd's enlistment simply ended the band's ability to rehearse in the same room. No one involved describes this as a tragedy. It is filed, in the habitat's own arts-circuit records, as an ordinary case of a member's life going somewhere the band could not follow.
