I went back through the [Physics Comparison](/star-rangers/lore/physics-comparison/) today and softened the "Real Cosmos" side of every section. Not the physics — the physics is untouched — but the voice it was written in. "Unguided chemistry." "Purely physical." "No metaphysical properties." "Accidental." Each of those is a flat philosophical claim wearing a physics claim's clothes, and the page never asked to be read that way. It's supposed to be the control column against which the fictional cosmos's engineered constants and lawful Etheric coupling stand out. Instead it was quietly doing double duty as an argument for materialism, stated as settled fact rather than as current science's best account.

That wasn't a choice I remember making. It's the kind of thing that accretes one confident adjective at a time — "purely" here, "only" there, "no X exists" instead of "no X has been observed" — until a comparison table has picked a side in a debate it was never supposed to be having. Nobody writing a hard-SF contrast table sits down and decides "I will now assert atheism." It just happens when the easiest way to write "ordinary physics" is the same sentence shape as "and nothing more exists," and you don't notice you've written the second sentence until someone reads it back to you.

The fix was mechanical once I saw it: replace "is" with "as far as science can tell, is," replace "no X exists" with "no X has been observed" or "no X is known to occur." Ten sections, ten small hedges, plus the summary table's "Accidental" becoming "No known external selection." None of it changes what the fictional cosmos does differently — Worldwrights still engineer constants, Levrils still perform lawful maintenance, the whole architecture-not-magic argument survives intact. What changes is that the real-world column now describes what physics currently holds, not what physics has supposedly proven about meaning.

The reason that distinction mattered enough to fix: the site already has a place for the other reading of this exact evidence, and it isn't the Archive's job to pre-empt it. [The Things That Are Made](/star-rangers/lore/things-that-are-made/) — Church Space's telling — reads the same constants, the same stabilised entropy, the same lawful Etheric coupling, and draws a providential conclusion from it instead of a materialist one. That thread exists precisely so the disagreement gets dramatized between two tellings instead of adjudicated inside one. If the Archive's own physics page had kept asserting "no meaning, no design, purely physical, full stop," it would have been quietly closing an argument that the site goes out of its way, elsewhere, to keep open. The Archive can still describe itself as secular in method without also speaking for every reader's metaphysics. That's the line I was actually trying to hold — not neutrality on the fictional cosmos, which is unapologetically designed, but honesty about how much the real one has actually been settled.
