A boundary zone is the region where two adjacent Concordants meet. Concordants are bounded regions of reality with internally consistent physical and Etheric conditions — but those conditions are not identical between any two Concordants. Where they touch, the conditions of each bleed into the other.

## What Happens at a Boundary Zone

The most immediately observable effect is **instrument drift**: measuring devices cannot agree on basic quantities (time, distance, local constants) because the quantities genuinely differ slightly on each side of the boundary. The instruments are all correct for their local position; the issue is that "local position" is ambiguous.

Other documented effects:
- **Resonance patterns in solid matter:** Etheric structures from one Concordant expressing through the material substrate of the other produce characteristic crystalline stress patterns.
- **Condensation and temperature anomalies:** Temperature differentials across the boundary produce physical effects with no internal explanation.
- **Visual shimmer:** At high boundary activity, the discontinuity becomes visible to humanoid perception as a shimmer or haze.
- **Ritual failure:** Practitioners working with Etheric structures find that the structures shift between measurement and application because the Etheric substrate on which they are working is itself unstable.

## Why They Are Studied

Boundary zones are scientifically significant because they are the only places where the effects of Concordant-level structure become directly observable at the Material layer. The survey corps maintains posts at boundary zones specifically to monitor for changes in the boundary's stability.

**Key rule for the story:** None of the boundary effects are supernatural. They are all lawful expressions of higher-layer structure — Etheric patterns expressing through physical limitation, Concordant-level rules governing what is possible in each region.

## Threshold Station and the Marsh Causeway

Threshold Station occupies a boundary zone where the chronometer discrepancy has persisted for at least eleven years. The Marsh Causeway is a surface-level site in the same boundary region where the discrepancy manifests as ritual interference and, possibly, as a feeding condition for Conceptual-layer predatory entities.

Whether these two locations are responding to the same underlying change in the boundary's stability is an open question as of Season 1.
