Ask a Cerebraun a simple question and the answer sometimes arrives in three parts, seconds apart, none of them contradicting the others. Xenolinguists spent a decade assuming this was evasion before admitting it was just how the species thinks.

The **Cerebraun** are a cephalopod-descended, sapient people distinguished from every singly-brained species on record by a single structural fact: cognition, for a Cerebraun, is not one thing happening in one place.

## Physical and Cognitive Characteristics

- A dominant central brain handles executive judgment, language, and long-term planning — the "voice" a Cerebraun presents to the outside world.
- Each of the species' eight primary limbs carries its own semi-autonomous ganglion, capable of independent sensory processing, reflexive decision-making, and — controversially, among xenobiologists who first documented it — a measurable degree of localized preference and mood.
- The central brain does not fully override the limb-ganglia. It negotiates with them. A Cerebraun under stress can visibly experience one limb's ganglion acting against the central brain's stated intention, a phenomenon their own physicians treat as ordinary rather than pathological.
- Skin bears chromatophore and texture-changing tissue capable of rapid, high-bandwidth display — used for identity signaling, emotional broadcast, and a formal display-language reserved for precise technical and diplomatic communication.

## Complex Inner Worlds

Outside observers who describe Cerebraun psychology as "layered" are, if anything, understating it. A single Cerebraun can hold what a human psychologist would classify as several simultaneous, only partially reconciled trains of affect and intention — not as internal conflict in the human sense, but as a normal condition of having cognition distributed across nine semi-independent processing centers instead of one.

This makes Cerebraun notoriously difficult for other species to model predictively. A human diplomat can learn to read a Krenyi's directness or a fellow human's tells. Predicting a Cerebraun requires tracking which of nine centers is currently weighted heaviest in a given exchange — information no outside observer has reliable access to, and which the Cerebraun themselves do not always resolve before they act.

## Communication

Cerebraun conversation operates on at least two simultaneous channels: spoken or written language carrying the central brain's stated position, and skin-display carrying real-time affective and confirmatory signal from the limb-ganglia beneath it. A trained Cerebraun listener reads both. Untrained outside observers — nearly everyone who isn't Cerebraun — reliably miss the second channel entirely, which is the single most-cited cause of cross-species misunderstanding in Hegemony diplomatic records.

## Social and Political Reflection

Cerebraun governance philosophy is, by the species' own account, a direct extension of their own neurology: legitimate authority is centralized in one dominant voice, and every subordinate part — whether a limb-ganglion or a subject administration — is expected to execute that voice's decisions rather than bargain over them as an equal. The [Cerebraun Hegemony](/star-rangers/lore/cerebraun-hegemony/) is that governing philosophy applied at civilizational scale.
