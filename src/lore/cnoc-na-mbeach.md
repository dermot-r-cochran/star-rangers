**Cnoc na mBeach** — Irish for "the hill of the bees" — is a hive-yard and a handful of stone cells on land no diocese claims and no Star Rangers Safety Corps district administers, founded the year after [Brother Fintan](/star-rangers/characters/brother-fintan/) retired from thirty-one years' boundary-safety service. It is not a monastery in any sense the [Fellowship of Light](/star-rangers/lore/fellowship-of-light/) or the [Monasteries of Mars](/star-rangers/lore/monasteries-of-mars/) would recognize as their own, and Fintan has never tried to make it one. It is simply the smallest possible unit the [Communion of the Called](/star-rangers/lore/communion-of-the-called/) has ever produced: one keeper, a hive-yard, and a standing door.

## No Charter, No Rule

Cnoc na mBeach keeps no rule of the kind a Fellowship chapter or a Martian hermitage writes down and hands to its novices. It has one habit that functions like one: whoever the Communion's chaplaincy rota sends through is fed, housed for as long as they need, and put to work at the hives whether they came for that or not. Fintan's stated reasoning is unremarkable — idle hands around an active hive are a hazard, not a courtesy — but the effect, over the years the rota has been running, has been to turn a retired Chief's private hobby into a way-stop every chaplain on the circuit eventually passes through at least once.

Nothing about the arrangement is formalized. The Communion itself keeps no charter and elects no equivalent of a Triumvirate; Cnoc na mBeach inherits that refusal by default rather than by any decision Fintan has ever made about it. A place that could be claimed by a diocese or folded into a Corps chaplaincy budget would stop being the place a cadet questioning her own posting can walk into unannounced, which is, more often than the rota strictly requires, exactly who walks in.

## The Wax

Every Communion way-chapel and ship's chaplaincy within reach of Cnoc na mBeach lights its services on wax rendered from Fintan's own hives — the arrangement he considers the entire justification for keeping more colonies than the honey alone would earn its keep. A hive tended well ends up lighting a service it will never attend and burning for a congregation it will never meet, a fact Fintan mentions often enough that visiting chaplains have started treating it as the hermitage's unofficial motto rather than an offhand remark.

## Telling the Bees

Cnoc na mBeach keeps the old custom of informing a hive directly of a death, a birth, or a departure in the household it belongs to — practiced on the reasoning, older than any instrument the Safety Corps ever certified, that a colony left uninformed about the household it belongs to will sicken or leave. Fintan has never claimed the custom does anything a Cascade reading would confirm, and keeps it anyway, on the same discipline [Brother Daire](/star-rangers/characters/brother-daire/) kept eight centuries before him: mark what is confirmed, mark what is practiced out of unbroken habit, and never blur the two.
