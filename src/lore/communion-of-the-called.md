The [Neutrality clause](/star-rangers/lore/formation-of-star-rangers/) binds the Corps, not the Ranger. It says the Rangers take no institutional side in a political dispute, and — by the same reading that gives the Fellowship's [ecumenical hymnody](/star-rangers/lore/arilon/) its neutral repertoire — no institutional side in whose liturgy is correct either. What it has never said, and was never written to say, is that a Ranger has to leave her own belief in the airlock before reporting for duty.

Most don't. A working majority of human Rangers who report any religious affiliation at all report a Christian one, in one of the traditions the [Celtic Union of Planets](/star-rangers/lore/celtic-union-of-planets/) carried off Earth or one its own colonists picked up since — [Saint Aoife's](/star-rangers/lore/saint-aoife/) devotion among them, though far from the only one. A smaller number of allied and partner species carry traditions the [Fellowship's comparative archive](/star-rangers/lore/arilon/) has independently filed close enough to the same shape to cross-reference: a called witness, a naming, a vindication that comes after. The Cascade is explicit that the shape recurring proves nothing about which tradition has it right. It has never stopped either side from recognising a neighbor when they meet one.

## What the Communion Is

The **Communion of the Called** is not a chapter, a corps, or anything the charter recognises. It is the working name for the actual network those congregations keep with each other: ship chapels, station parishes, and a chaplaincy rota that moves with postings rather than staying fixed to one installation, tying human and non-human congregations together across a distance no single denomination's own hierarchy was built to span. No Ranger rank sits inside it. A High Captain and a Cadet attend the same station chapel on the same footing, because the Communion's only qualification for belonging is showing up.

It keeps no charter, elects no equivalent of a Triumvirate, and — deliberately, in a structure that mirrors the [Monasteries of Mars'](/star-rangers/lore/monasteries-of-mars/) own refusal to formalise — has declined every proposal to become one. A network that could be captured by a single chain of command would stop being safe for a Cadet and a High Captain to sit in on the same footing.

## Why the Corps Leaves It Alone

The Neutrality clause could have been read to prohibit this — Rangers gathering, off duty, under one tradition's name, station chapels appearing on the same deck plans as the mess and the infirmary. It wasn't, and the Corps' own Provost track has the settled interpretation on file: the clause binds what the *Corps* institutionally endorses, not what its people do on their own time and their own initiative. A chaplain who serves aboard a Ranger vessel serves at the invitation of that vessel's crew, draws no Corps rank, and answers to the Communion, not to the Starwarden. The distinction is the same one that lets a Ranger vote in a planetary election as a private citizen while the Corps itself endorses no candidate — personal conviction was never the thing Neutrality was written to constrain.
