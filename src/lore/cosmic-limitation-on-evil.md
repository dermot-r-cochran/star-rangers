Ask an Archive theorist whether the Cascade punishes evil and, for decades, the answer was the same: no, that's a category error. The Cosmic Limitation on Evil is the one confirmed exception, and the Archive has never been fully comfortable with it.

The **Cosmic Limitation on Evil** is a law designed by [Kieme](/star-rangers/glossary/kieme/): every entity that exists in service of evil will dissolve and fade once it no longer serves any higher purpose. Unlike Kieme's other nonlocal constraints, Archive material does not describe this one as merely inferred from an absence of counterexamples — it is described as *designed*, a deliberate act of the Upper Structure rather than a passive boundary condition.

## The Constraint

No entity, institution, pattern, or persistent effect that exists in service of evil can outlast the higher purpose it was bent toward. Once that purpose is exhausted — once the harm stops serving whatever end justified the Cascade tolerating it at all — the entity dissolves and fades. Predatory and parasitic beings that feed on suffering are the clearest documented case: see [Predatory Entities](/star-rangers/lore/predatory-entities/) for what happens once one outlives its purpose.

The dissolution is not swift, and it is not merciful. It is owed.

## Cosmic Debt

All evil and all suffering, in Archive doctrine, creates a **cosmic debt** — an imbalance the universe carries until it is repaid. Kieme is credited with repaying it, on whatever schedule and by whatever mechanism Kieme alone determines. No confirmed instance of a cosmic debt going permanently unpaid has ever been documented.

Neither the timeline nor the mechanism of repayment is specified anywhere in canon Archive material. That silence is treated as doctrine, not evasion: a debt whose repayment could be predicted would stop functioning as a warning to whoever is currently running one up.

## The Exception the Archive Keeps Explaining Away

The [Cascade's Global Invariants](/star-rangers/lore/cosmic-cascade/) state, flatly, that there is no moral cosmology — no virtue, no sin, no reward, no punishment. The Cosmic Limitation on Evil is the one law that reads, on its face, like exactly that. Successive generations of Archive theorists have tried to rephrase it as structural rather than moral — load-bearing capacity, not judgment — and every rephrasing has struggled to explain why the law only ever targets evil and never anything else. Devotional tradition has never needed the rephrasing. It has simply called the Limitation what it looks like: Kieme keeping the ledger.
