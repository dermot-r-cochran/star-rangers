Ask an Archive theorist whether the Cascade punishes evil and the answer is always the same: no, and that is the wrong question. The right question is why nothing built entirely from harm has ever been documented outlasting its usefulness.

The **Cosmic Limitation on Evil** is a nonlocal constraint Archive theorists attribute to [Kieme](/star-rangers/glossary/kieme/), inferred by the same method used for every other Upper Structure constraint: not because anyone has observed Kieme act, but because the alternative has never once been confirmed, across any [Concordant](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant/).

## The Constraint

No configuration — entity, institution, pattern, or persistent effect — sustained primarily by inflicted harm can borrow structural support from the Cascade indefinitely. Once such a configuration stops serving any function further constraint-propagation requires, that support ends, and the configuration dissolves. Predatory and parasitic beings that feed on suffering are the clearest documented case: see [Predatory Entities](/star-rangers/lore/predatory-entities/) for what happens once one outlives the niche that let it persist.

The dissolution is not swift, and it is not merciful. It is simply reliable.

## Cosmic Debt

Archive language gives a name to what accumulates in the meantime: **cosmic debt**, the unresolved imbalance left behind by harm and suffering that has not yet stopped serving its purpose. Theorists attribute its eventual repayment to Kieme, on the same inferential basis as the Limitation itself — no confirmed instance of a cosmic debt going permanently unpaid has ever been documented.

Neither the timeline nor the mechanism of repayment is specified anywhere in canon Archive material. Devotional sources fill the gap freely. The Archive does not.

## What This Is Not

This is not the [Cascade's moral cosmology](/star-rangers/lore/cosmic-cascade/) reasserting itself under a different name. The Cascade's Global Invariants are explicit: no virtue, no sin, no reward, no punishment. The Limitation does not describe judgment — it describes load-bearing capacity. A pattern dissolves because the structure stops holding it up, not because it has been found guilty of anything. Archive theorists who blur that distinction are, without exception, working from devotional rather than technical sources.
