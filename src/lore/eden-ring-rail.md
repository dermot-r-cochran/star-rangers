Most visitors to Eden never think about the Ring-Rail until they miss it. It is not the fold route, not the Compact, not any of the things that get a habitat's name into a Star Rangers briefing — it is the commuter line that gets a dockworker from the outer residential arc to a shift change on time, and it runs so reliably that residents mostly notice it by complaining when it doesn't.

## Layout

The Ring-Rail runs the inner rim of Eden's spin ring, the same structural band that carries most of the habitat's residential and commercial decking, circling the full ring rather than cutting across it. A separate, much shorter set of radial spoke-cars connects the ring to the low-gravity hub — docking, cargo transfer, and the administrative and scientific offices clustered nearest the [Eden Passage](/star-rangers/lore/eden-space-habitat/) monitoring stations — but the spokes are a connector, not a commute. The Ring-Rail is where Eden's daily life actually happens: it is the habitat's subway, running under full spin gravity the whole way round, with no leg of the ordinary trip ever asking a rider to adjust to weightlessness.

## Who Runs It

The Ring-Rail is scheduled and dispatched by the [Eden Warden](/star-rangers/characters/eden-warden/) — specifically Ward, the front-facing half of the collective, in what is by volume the single largest piece of routine administrative work either persona handles. It is unglamorous work by the Warden's own account, and also, Ward has noted in more than one habitat-council filing, the fastest way to know something is wrong on Eden before any incident report reaches a desk: a delay pattern that doesn't match maintenance logs, a car running under capacity on a shift that should fill it, a platform sensor flagging a crowd where none was scheduled. The Warden has never needed to say this loudly. Mayor [Petrakis](/star-rangers/characters/imogen-petrakis/)'s office has quietly come to treat an unscheduled Ring-Rail service change as an early warning worth returning a call for, even when Ward's filing gives no reason beyond "recommend attention."

## Funding and the Two Offices

Who pays for the Ring-Rail, and who answers when a fare increase or a service cut becomes a habitat-council agenda item, runs straight into the same dual line that governs the rest of Eden's civil administration. Mayor Petrakis's office treats it as a resident-facing utility, funded and defended on that basis; Commissioner [Wayland](/star-rangers/characters/zara-wayland/)'s office, answering upward to the [Orbital Habitats Compact](/star-rangers/lore/orbital-habitats-compact/), has periodically floated Compact-wide standardisation of fare and maintenance schedules across all five member habitats, on the reasoning that a shared parts and scheduling framework would be cheaper than five habitats solving the same rolling-stock problem separately. Petrakis has not refused this outright. She has also never let a standardisation proposal move faster than her own council's review of it, on the same general principle that has kept her re-elected: a habitat utility answers to the people riding it first, and to Compact-wide efficiency second.

## The Hub-Ward Restriction

One stretch of track is not like the rest. The innermost spoke-cars terminating nearest the Eden Passage monitoring stations require clearance that ordinary Ring-Rail fare does not grant — a restriction set by the Survey Corps and Solar System Defence Command's joint oversight of the Passage itself, not by the Compact or by Ward. It is the one place Eden's transit system visibly touches the habitat's other chain of authority, the one [Rangers otherwise have no jurisdiction to reach into](/star-rangers/lore/military-space-command/): a Ranger with Passage-relevant business can ride that spoke car, and no other part of the Compact's civil transit, without the two chains needing to consult each other about it. Ward enforces the clearance check without holding an opinion on it either way — a rare instance of the Warden declining to file a recommendation on something in its own operational purview, on the stated grounds that jurisdiction is not a maintenance question.
