Eden was built to be forgettable: a civilian habitat set where supply lines were steady and traffic was thin. Then its instruments began to disagree with the sky.

Eden Space Habitat was commissioned in 2671 UCSD as a civilian deep-range research and habitation platform, meant for long-duration study of the outer boundary between the Solar System's primary [Concordant](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant/) and the adjacent boundary zone. By every model available to the MSC survey office, its location was ordinary.

It was not ordinary.

## The Fold Route Discovery

Before Eden, higher-dimensional fold routes inside the Solar System had been theorized but not confirmed. The theory was sound. The problem was detection.

Localizing a route required either better instrumentation than the MSC possessed or a habitat close enough to live inside the drift signature long enough to notice the pattern. Eden supplied the second condition by accident.

Within three years, Eden's staff were filing reports of fold-adjacent anomalies: dual-valid positional readings, periodic navigation drift, and optical events in which two points in space briefly seemed to coincide. The first four reports were dismissed as sensor degradation.

The fifth, cross-correlated across six independent instruments, was dismissed as a shared calibration error.

The sixth sat unread for eleven months.

By the time the MSC formed a committee, Eden's chief scientist, Dr. Ysolde Maren Pitch, had already completed a 340-page analysis identifying the source as a natural higher-dimensional fold route passing within 0.003 AU of the habitat. She submitted it as supplementary material. The committee cited it in a footnote fourteen months later.

## The Unauthorised Expeditions

Dr. Pitch's analysis also contained a navigation model for entering and traversing the route using modified Eden hardware.

She submitted that as well. The MSC classified it as proprietary research pending operational review and instructed Eden's administration not to disseminate it.

The instruction was filed. In practice, it could not be enforced. Dr. Pitch was the habitat's senior scientific authority, and the MSC monitoring officer lacked both technical competence in fold mechanics and a chain of command capable of acting quickly.

So the first unauthorised expedition launched.

In 2712 UCSD, the modified cargo hauler *Constant Margin* traversed the route with a crew of three and returned with navigational data proving the corridor led to a point approximately 1.4 light-years outside the Solar System. It was the first confirmed extrasolar transit in human history.

In 2714 UCSD, the six-person *Patience First* followed. It did not return. Its fate remained unconfirmed for another eighteen years.

## The MSC Response

The MSC responded to *Constant Margin* by classifying the data, assigning three more monitoring officers, and expanding Eden's compliance framework by twelve requirements.

It responded to the disappearance of *Patience First* by opening an inquiry, assigning that inquiry to a committee, and noting that the expedition had been unauthorized and therefore beyond MSC liability.

What the MSC could not do quickly—because its structure was not built to do it—was recognize that an inhabited habitat beside a natural extrasolar fold route demanded a different order of institution.

The route did not wait for committee calendars. The boundary zone at its far end did not pause for compliance review. Whatever had happened to *Patience First* was happening beyond the edge of the Solar System's [Concordant](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant/), where MSC authority stopped and procedural confidence became memory.

## Eden's Role in the Founding of the Star Rangers

Eden's fold-adjacent position, combined with the MSC's documented inability to respond on operational timescales, became the clearest factual case for creating the Star Rangers.

The founding charter cited Eden directly: *a permanent inhabited platform at fold-route proximity requires a dedicated, independent operational capacity with authority and response time adequate to the environment — not a committee, not a compliance framework, and not a monitoring officer with no chain of command that reaches the boundary.*

Eden remains active. It is now designated a Class I Fold-Proximate Research Platform under joint oversight from the Star Rangers' Survey Corps and the Solar System Defence Command, the institution that replaced the MSC after the Consolidation Accords. That oversight governs the fold route. Eden's ordinary civil administration and policing runs on a separate, parallel chain — the [Orbital Habitats Compact](/star-rangers/lore/orbital-habitats-compact/) — that answers to neither the Survey Corps nor the SSDC at all.

The route is now mapped, monitored, and named: the Eden Passage.

*Patience First* was found in 2732 UCSD. The crew were alive. The classified record contains the full account. Publicly, only this is stated: they were found by entities who were not human, not Krenyi, and not mechanical—and who had been waiting for someone to arrive.
