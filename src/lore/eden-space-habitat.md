Eden Space Habitat was commissioned in 2671 UCSD as a civilian deep-range research and habitation platform, intended to support long-duration study of the outer boundary between the Solar System's primary [Concordant](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant/) and the adjacent boundary zone. Its placement was chosen for stability of supply lines and distance from high-traffic shipping corridors. It was, by every metric available to the MSC survey office in 2671, an unremarkable position.

It was not an unremarkable position.

## The Fold Route Discovery

Higher-dimensional fold routes — natural topological corridors that permit transit by compressing effective spatial distance — were theorised but not confirmed within the Solar System prior to Eden's construction. The theoretical models predicted they would exist, but localising them required either instrumentation that the MSC did not yet possess or long-term habitation close enough to a route's emergence signature to detect the associated instrument drift.

Eden provided the second option by accident.

Within three years of occupancy, Eden's research staff began filing reports of instrument anomalies consistent with fold-adjacent interference: dual-valid positional readings, periodic short-duration coherence drift in navigation systems, and a set of optical observations that the resident astronomer described as "apparent spatial coincidence artefacts" — two points in space that briefly appeared to occupy the same location. The first four reports were reviewed by the MSC's instrument calibration board and attributed to sensor degradation.

The fifth report included data from six independently-sourced instruments, cross-correlated, showing the same anomaly signature at 48-hour intervals, and was reviewed by the MSC's instrument calibration board and attributed to a shared calibration error introduced during Eden's last maintenance cycle.

The sixth report was not reviewed for eleven months.

By the time the MSC formed an investigative committee, Eden's chief scientist, Dr. Ysolde Maren Pitch, had already produced a 340-page analysis identifying the anomaly source as a natural higher-dimensional fold route passing within 0.003 AU of Eden's current position. She submitted it to the MSC committee as supplementary material. The committee's preliminary report, released fourteen months later, cited her analysis in a footnote.

## The Unauthorised Expeditions

Dr. Pitch's analysis included a navigation model for entering and traversing the fold route using existing Eden platform technology with modifications she had designed. She submitted the model to the MSC committee. The committee classified it as proprietary research under pending operational review and instructed Eden's administration to ensure it was not disseminated pending formal assessment.

The instruction was noted and filed. It was not enforceable in a habitat where Dr. Pitch had been the most senior scientific authority for six years and where the MSC's monitoring officer had no technical background in fold mechanics and reported to a command layer that could not act on his reports without committee approval.

The first unauthorised expedition — a modified cargo hauler designated *Constant Margin* with a crew of three — traversed the fold route in 2712 UCSD and returned with navigational data confirming that the route connected to a transit point approximately 1.4 light-years outside the Solar System. This was the first confirmed extrasolar transit in human history.

The second expedition — six crew, designated *Patience First* — traversed the route in 2714 UCSD and did not return. The nature of their fate was not confirmed for another eighteen years.

## The MSC Response

The MSC's formal response to the *Constant Margin* expedition was to classify its navigational data, assign three additional monitoring officers to Eden, and add twelve new compliance requirements to the habitat's operational framework.

Its response to the *Patience First* disappearance was to open a formal inquiry, assign the inquiry to a committee, and note that the expedition had been conducted without authorisation and therefore without MSC liability.

What the MSC did not do — what its structure made it constitutionally unable to do quickly — was recognise that the discovery of a natural extrasolar fold route near an inhabited habitat was a category of event that required a different kind of institutional response than the ones it had.

The fold route did not care about compliance frameworks. The boundary zone at its terminus did not pause for committee review. Whatever had happened to *Patience First* was happening in a place where the MSC's authority ran out at the edge of the Solar System's [Concordant](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant/), and its monitoring protocols stopped at the edge of Eden's docking bay.

## Eden's Role in the Founding of the Star Rangers

The combination of Eden's fold-adjacent position and the MSC's demonstrable inability to respond to it in operationally relevant timeframes became the primary factual basis for the arguments, eventually successful, that led to the founding of the Star Rangers.

The founding charter cited Eden specifically: *a permanent inhabited platform at fold-route proximity requires a dedicated, independent operational capacity with authority and response time adequate to the environment — not a committee, not a compliance framework, and not a monitoring officer with no chain of command that reaches the boundary.*

Eden Space Habitat remains active. It is now designated a Class I Fold-Proximate Research Platform and operates under joint oversight from the Star Rangers' Survey Corps and the Solar System Defence Command, the institution that replaced the MSC after the Consolidation Accords.

The fold route is mapped, monitored, and named: the Eden Passage.

*Patience First* was found in 2732 UCSD. The crew were alive. The full account of what they had encountered at the fold route's terminus is in the Survey Corps classified record. What can be stated publicly is that they were found by entities who were not human, not Krenyi, and not mechanical — and who had been waiting for someone to arrive.
