Reality is expressed through five layers. These layers are causal modes, not metaphorical planes.

## Layer 1 — Spiritual

### Definition
Origin condition without personhood or agency.

### Mechanism
Provides existence boundary conditions for all lower expressions.

### Constraints
- No dialogue, intention, or intervention semantics.

### Observable Effects
- Only inferred through invariants that persist across all Concordants.

## Layer 2 — Conceptual

### Definition
Identity and meaning structures before embodiment.

### Mechanism
Coherence patterns form admissible concept states.

### Constraints
- No direct physical action without lower-layer coupling.

### Observable Effects
- Stable meaning-pattern recurrence across distinct cultures.

## Layer 3 — Etheric

### Definition
Rule-template layer governing what physical expression can remain coherent.

### Mechanism
Archetypal templates constrain physical and material admissibility.

### Constraints
- Etheric templates cannot contradict Cascade hierarchy constraints.

### Observable Effects
- Persisting pattern behavior after local physical triggers are removed.

## Layer 4 — Physical

### Definition
Spacetime, fields, and energy interactions.

### Mechanism
Physical law runs locally under Concordant boundary conditions.

### Constraints
- Boundary zones produce lawful disagreement, not law failure.

### Observable Effects
- Instrument drift and local-constant variance near interfaces.

## Layer 5 — Material

### Definition
Matter, biology, technology, and civilization.

### Mechanism
Material systems express lower-layer constraints through actionable processes.

### Constraints
- Material-layer practice cannot override higher-layer constraints.

### Observable Effects
- Operational success correlates with constraint literacy.

## Cross-Layer Interaction Rules

- No magic: every cross-layer effect requires lawful coupling.
- Universe overlap/folding modifies geometry and observables, not hierarchy.
- Archetypal interference is a measurable Etheric→Material transfer pattern.
