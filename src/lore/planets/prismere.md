Most survey reports note a world's ring system as a footnote of orbital mechanics. Prismere's first full Federation survey put it in the summary paragraph, because on a clear night the rings are the least strange thing in the sky.

**Prismere** is the third planet of the **Iridess System** and a charter member world of the [Federation of Sentient Beings](/star-rangers/lore/federation-of-sentient-beings/). It is a genuine biosphere world in the same technical sense as [Tír na nÓg](/star-rangers/lore/planets/tir-na-nog/) or [Verdance](/star-rangers/lore/planets/verdance/) — stable, self-sustaining, no life-support engineering required — but its biosphere runs on a silicate-carbon chemistry that builds glass and crystal into living tissue the way Earth-descended life builds cellulose and chitin. The result is a world that produces its own light. Prismere's forests glow before the local sun sets, and after dark the ambient bioluminescence is bright enough to read a datapad by from most points on the surface.

## The Lattice

![A biomechanical aerial creature gliding beneath two ringed planets and a scatter of moons, with luminous jellyfish-like fauna and an armored ground crawler in the foreground](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-orbital-vista.jpg)

Iridess is a crowded system — a dense ring plane, seven moons across Prismere's own orbit, and two neighboring ringed worlds close enough in-system to be visible as more than points of light. That gravitational crowding does something [Lagrange Fold Points and Fold-Space Transit](/star-rangers/lore/lagrange-fold-points/) predicts but rarely delivers so visibly: it produces an unusually dense confluence of stable [Quantum Space Harmonic](/star-rangers/lore/quantum-space-harmonics/) corridors passing directly through the system, at a density the Survey Corps has not catalogued anywhere else in charted space.

Prismere's upper atmosphere carries a permanent haze of suspended crystalline particulate, seeded continuously by its own biosphere. That haze does what no instrument-grade sensor array does on its own: it refracts the harmonic corridors' field geometry into visible light, tracing faint, mathematically precise lines, arcs, and polygons across the night sky wherever a corridor's resonant topology currently sits. Survey Corps astrogators call the phenomenon **the Lattice**. It shifts, slowly and predictably, as corridors open, hold, and collapse — a naked-eye readout of fold-space conditions that would otherwise require a full sensor suite to reconstruct.

![A silvery, biomechanical winged organism flying beneath swirling green-and-purple sky patterns, with pale crystalline coral fauna below](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-twilight-lattice.jpg)

No other surveyed world sits close enough to a comparable corridor confluence and carries the right atmospheric chemistry to make the Lattice visible. Federation and Survey Corps consensus is that Prismere is not unique in having dense fold geometry overhead — only in having a sky that shows it.

## Glasswood and the Luminous Biosphere

Prismere's dominant flora is **glasswood**: slow-growing, crystalline-barked trees whose translucent foliage doubles as a lens, catching ambient Lattice light and starlight alike and re-emitting it in the soft, shifting color washes that make Prismere's forests visible from orbit after dark.

![A cluster of translucent, glowing flower-like growths on a crystalline stalk beneath a banded gas giant](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-glasswood-grove.jpg)

![A luminous flowering glasswood stand at night, with small many-legged foraging fauna moving across the ground beneath it](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-flowering-glasswood.jpg)

Mineral analogues to fungal towers — Prismeri call them **lumenspires** — rise from glasswood-grove floors in loose colonies, each spire's internal bioluminescence fed by a shared root-mat network rather than by any individual growth. Prismeri settlements are built into rather than cleared from lumenspire stands, in the same register documented for Meridian's reef-cities: architecture that reads, to an untrained eye, as a natural formation right up until someone notices the load-bearing structure has been shaped.

![A colony of glowing, amber-lit lumenspire towers rising from a crystalline grove, with a jellyfish-like aerial creature drifting past](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-luminous-towers.jpg)

Taller, lattice-veined structures of fused silica — locally called **glass spires** — rise from Prismere's higher elevations independent of any glasswood grove. Survey botanists still debate whether glass spires are a wholly separate crystalline organism or an extreme adult growth form of glasswood; the two do not currently interbreed by any confirmed mechanism, but their internal mineral structure is close enough that the question remains open.

![Tall, translucent glass spire structures against a night sky showing a visible spiral galaxy, with crystalline reef-like fauna at ground level](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-glass-spires.jpg)

## Native Fauna

Prismere's low gravity and dense, particulate-rich atmosphere support a genuinely aerial ecosystem, not merely a flying one. **Driftjellies** — translucent, gas-bladder fauna that spend their entire adult lives aloft — ride thermals off the lumenspire groves in loose drifting colonies, trailing luminous feeding tendrils that filter airborne spore and particulate matter. They are harmless, common, and by far the most-photographed native animal on the world.

![Three luminous, jellyfish-like driftjellies floating above armored, spike-shelled ground fauna beneath a golden ring system](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-driftjellies.jpg)

Ground level belongs to **thornbacks**, armored, crystal-spined grazing fauna that browse glasswood undergrowth and shelter in lumenspire root-shadow during the brightest hours of the local day. Their shells are Prismere's most commonly exported novelty mineral good, shed naturally during growth molts and collected rather than harvested from a living animal.

Prismere's apex aerial predator is the **umbral ray** — a large, silent glider with a biomechanical-looking wing structure adapted for hunting driftjelly colonies and, on rare confirmed occasions, unwary Prismeri who stray from lumenspire settlements after dark without a Lattice-reader's escort. Umbral rays hunt by riding the same thermals driftjellies do, closing in total silence; Prismeri settlements univerally observe a dusk curfew for exactly this reason.

![A large, silvery, biomechanical-looking flying creature gliding through a dim sky, with spiny armored fauna and pale crystalline plants on the ground below](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismere-umbral-ray.jpg)

## The Prismeri and the Federation

Prismere is the homeworld of the [Prismeri](/star-rangers/lore/prismeri/), whose biological sensitivity to the Lattice's harmonic geometry — a byproduct of the same silicate-carbon physiology that built their world's glowing biosphere — makes them the Federation's most valued fold-corridor readers. Where [Aspenar](/star-rangers/lore/planets/aspenar/) underwrites the Federation's Ranger support compact in refined material and [Verdance](/star-rangers/lore/planets/verdance/) anchors it in diplomatic standing, Prismere's contribution is expertise: Prismeri astrogation guilds provide independent corridor-condition confirmation that Survey Corps sensor arrays alone cannot fully replicate, a service formalized under the same standing compact that moves Aspenar's alloy stock toward Ranger fabrication yards.
