Federation contact teams filed two separate preliminary species reports on their first survey of Prismere, a full local year apart, before a frustrated field xenobiologist cross-referenced kinship records and proved both reports described the same person at two points in one life.

The **Prismeri** are a sapient people native to [Prismere](/star-rangers/lore/planets/prismere/), built from the same silicate-carbon biochemistry that gives their homeworld's forests their glow. They are distinguished from every other catalogued Federation species by an unusually dramatic single metamorphosis: every Prismeri is born, grows, and lives for decades in one body plan, then undergoes an irreversible physical transformation into a second, structurally distinct adult form — a single life stage change so severe that early survey teams reasonably mistook it for two related species sharing one world.

## First Wings

![A slender, grey-violet winged humanoid with gossamer wings and a segmented skull ridge, standing beneath a dark moon and a distant ringed planet](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismeri-first-wings.jpg)

A Prismeri is born and spends the first several decades of life in what is called the **First Wings** form: slender, light-boned, with translucent gossamer wing membranes and a soft, still-hardening exoskeleton in grey-violet tones. First Wings Prismeri are fully sapient and legally adult by their own culture's reckoning well before metamorphosis — the form is not a childhood stage in the way it first appeared to outside observers, only an early adult one. First Wings individuals fill most of Prismeri society's exploratory, apprentice, and scouting roles: their lighter frame and lower stall speed make them better suited to close-quarters glasswood-grove navigation than the form they will eventually grow into.

## The Second Emergence

At a point in adulthood that varies by individual — triggered, current Federation xenobiology holds, by a combination of accumulated silicate-mineral intake and prolonged exposure to concentrated Lattice geometry — a Prismeri enters **the Second Emergence**: a molt-like transformation, taking place over several local weeks inside a self-secreted mineral shell, from which the individual emerges in **Full Wings** form.

![A gold-and-blue chitin-plated winged humanoid with long sensory tendrils, standing among crystalline flora beneath a banded planet](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismeri-full-wings.jpg)

Full Wings Prismeri are structurally distinct enough from First Wings that the mistaken two-species classification persisted through two Federation survey passes: hardened, gold-and-blue chitin plating replaces the soft grey-violet exoskeleton, the gossamer wing membranes are replaced with stronger, load-bearing wings capable of sustained high-altitude flight, and a set of long, motile sensory tendrils grow from the skull ridge, dense with the same crystalline-mineral structures that make the Lattice visible in Prismere's sky. Those tendrils give Full Wings Prismeri a biological sensitivity to ambient harmonic-corridor geometry that no First Wings individual — and no known instrument — can match, which is why Full Wings adults, not First Wings apprentices, crew Prismeri astrogation guilds.

The Second Emergence is irreversible and, by Prismeri account, not universal: a meaningful fraction of Prismeri live a full natural lifespan in First Wings form without ever undergoing it, and Prismeri culture accords no lesser standing to those who don't. Federation medical missions have logged interest in the trigger mechanism for decades; Prismeri astrogation guilds, whose institutional value rests entirely on Full Wings sensory capability, have been consistently, politely unhelpful to that research.

## Society and the Lattice

Prismeri social structure follows the practical difference between the two forms rather than any formal caste system: First Wings individuals scout, apprentice, and tend lumenspire settlements, while Full Wings individuals — who account for a minority of the adult population — crew the astrogation guilds that read Prismere's sky for the Federation and, through the standing Federation-Ranger material compact, for Survey Corps fold-route confirmation work.

![Two Prismeri, one First Wings and one Full Wings, facing each other beneath a crystalline geometric sky pattern, with luminous jellyfish-like fauna nearby](/star-rangers/images/lore/prismeri-lattice-gathering.jpg)

Guild status is not hereditary and cannot be, since no Prismeri chooses or predicts whether the Second Emergence will ever come for them; a First Wings individual who never transforms simply never qualifies for guild work, regardless of standing or ambition, and Prismeri institutions treat that outcome as a fact of biology rather than a verdict on the person. That structural humility — no one earns the transformation, no one is denied it by any authority — is, Prismeri cultural historians argue, the reason guild status never hardened into the kind of resented hereditary privilege it might have elsewhere.

## Interspecies Notes

Prismeri cannot interbreed with humans or any other known Federation species, and the silicate-mineral component of their physiology cannot be transplanted or replicated in a non-Prismeri host, despite recurring Federation medical interest paralleling the standing interest in [Verdani](/star-rangers/lore/verdani/) thallenwort symbiosis. Prismeri tolerate standard Federation humanoid life-support and atmospheric conditions without difficulty, but require periodic return to Prismere-equivalent ambient light spectrum and, for Full Wings individuals specifically, some exposure to genuine harmonic-corridor geometry — a standing accommodation written into every off-world Prismeri posting contract, Ranger liaison postings included, since prolonged deprivation of either is documented to cause a measurable decline in Full Wings sensory acuity.
