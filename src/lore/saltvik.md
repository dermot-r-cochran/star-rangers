Ask a Knarrheim delegate which Line settlement actually keeps the Solar System's shipyards supplied, and the answer is never Knarrheim itself.

**Saltvik** — "salt bay," a name its founders kept from a home-village fishing cove none of them ever expected to see again — is a working cluster of six major ore-refinery platforms and dozens of smaller claim-tenders, threaded through a metal-rich rubble field the Line has worked continuously since the cooperative's earliest Belt surveys. Where [Knarrheim](/star-rangers/lore/knarrheim/) grew into the Line's political and cultural centre almost by accident, Saltvik has never been anything but what it was built for: it is the Line's largest single source of refined ore, and the settlement every other Line habitat depends on more than it likes to publicly admit.

## The Rubble Field

Saltvik's claim sits inside an unusually dense rubble cluster rather than a single large body — a geological accident that makes the field simultaneously rich and dangerous to work, since no two claim-tenders' charts of it fully agree from one survey season to the next. Line pilots train on Saltvik's approach lanes specifically because they are considered the hardest routine navigation task in Belt-registered space; a tender crew that has worked a full season at Saltvik is, by unspoken but universal Line custom, never questioned on their piloting again anywhere else in the fleet.

## Six Platforms, One Bell

Saltvik's six refinery platforms operate under separate local foremen but share a single, deliberately old-fashioned safety custom: a physical bell, mounted at the cluster's central relay platform and rung by hand, that signals an all-stop across every platform in the field simultaneously the instant any one crew calls a hazard. Automated hazard broadcasts exist and are used for routine coordination, but the bell — original to the settlement's earliest years, replaced twice, never retired — is kept specifically because every platform's crew has been trained since childhood to stop first and check the automated feed second. Line safety records credit the custom with more prevented incidents than any single piece of Saltvik's considerably more modern hazard-detection equipment.

## People and Custom

Saltvik's population skews younger and more transient than Knarrheim's settled residential rings — claim-tender and refinery work draws Line crews for a working generation rather than a lifetime, and it is common for a family with deep Knarrheim roots to send its newest crew generation to Saltvik first, on the understanding that a season worked there is closer to a rite of passage than an ordinary posting. Swedish predominates as the working language on the refinery floor, a demographic accident of which founding cooperatives originally staffed the cluster, though Saltvik's common-room signage stays deliberately multilingual out of long habit. The settlement sends its full delegate allotment to [the Thing](/star-rangers/lore/knarr-line/) every session it convenes, and Saltvik delegates are, by reputation across the rest of the Line, the ones least inclined to leave a disputed allocation formula unchallenged.
