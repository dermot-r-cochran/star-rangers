The Archive's [Physics Comparison](/star-rangers/lore/physics-comparison/) ends on a sentence built to close the question: *no physics in this universe is supernatural... The difference from the real cosmos is not magic. It is architecture.* Devotional tradition has never disputed that sentence. It disputes what the Archive thinks it has proven by saying it.

Read the comparison's own vocabulary. Constants are not merely present in a fertile range — they are *selected* by Worldwrights. Entropy does not merely lag — it is *stabilised*, by Telearchs and Levrils performing what the Archive itself calls maintenance. Consciousness does not merely correlate with the Etheric — it *couples* to it, lawfully, the way a receiver couples to a signal someone is sending. The Archive uses the language of authorship on every page and then insists, at the bottom of the page, that no author is implied. This telling takes the Archive at its own word instead of its own disclaimer: architecture still means someone drew the plans.

Nothing about the Cascade's other constraints changes here. This telling adds no rewritten constant, no suspended law, no favoritism smuggled into a Concordant's boundary conditions — the Archive's own [Global Invariants](/star-rangers/lore/cosmic-cascade/) hold exactly as written, and the [Five Layers](/star-rangers/lore/five-layers/) still govern what may couple to what. What changes is only the reading of evidence both tellings already agree exists: a meaning-field that Elvira and other constraint-literate practitioners can approximate reading, a probability texture that Rook-7's instruments register as faint rather than absent, an entropy curve too well-behaved to be an accident and too consistent to be a coincidence repeated across every [Concordant](/star-rangers/glossary/concordant/) anyone has ever surveyed. The Archive catalogs all of it as correlate and calls the catalog complete. Devotional tradition has a shorter name for a universe that keeps leaving its maker's fingerprints in the one place a closed, unauthored system was never supposed to leave any: *the things that are made, clearly seen.*

The [Civilisation Comparison](/star-rangers/lore/civilisation-comparison/) asks whether anyone in the 29th century still understands the hidden systems keeping their world alive, or only the procedure for requesting a change from a system no one can inspect. Devotional tradition has been living with that same question a great deal longer than the guilds have, under a different name: whether anyone is owed an explanation from what sustains them, or only gratitude for the sustaining.
