A xenobiologist's first Verdani skin sample usually produces the same two-part surprise: it is not skin pigment causing the color, and the organisms causing it are not passengers.

The **Verdani** are a sapient people native to [Verdance](/star-rangers/lore/planets/verdance/), distinguished from every other catalogued species by an obligate symbiotic relationship with **thallenwort** — a photosynthetic microflora that colonizes specialized dermal structures called **kinbeds** from early infancy and remains for life.

## Symbiosis, Not Cosmetic

Thallenwort is not decorative. A Verdani's kinbed colonies supplement the individual's own metabolism with a measurable share of daily caloric intake through direct photosynthetic transfer, meaning diet, sun exposure, and thallenwort health are not separable questions for Verdani physicians the way they would be for an unrelated species living alongside a decorative microorganism. A Verdani whose kinbeds sicken is not cosmetically affected; they are metabolically compromised, and Verdani medicine treats kinbed health with the same seriousness a human physician gives cardiac health.

Thallenwort strains are not uniform. Distinct strains, adapted over generations to the light quality, humidity, and canopy density of one of Verdance's four biomes, produce distinct coloration and mild metabolic differences in their hosts — which is why canopy-belt and steppe Verdani, at first Federation contact, presented distinctly enough that survey teams initially catalogued them as related but separate species before confirmed interfertility closed the question.

## Kinstrain and Naming

A Verdani's thallenwort strain is inherited, not chosen — passed from parent to child through direct kinbed-to-kinbed contact in infancy, a bonding process Verdani culture treats as the foundational kinship act, more binding in practice than shared residence or even shared parentage without it. A child raised by kin who could not complete kinbed-bonding is, in every legal and cultural sense that matters on Verdance, still that family's child; a child who received another lineage's strain through bonding, regardless of birth parentage, belongs to the strain-lineage that bonded them.

This is the origin of the Verdani naming convention outside observers usually notice first: a personal name followed by a kinstrain name — Farrowkin, Duskkin, Rootkin, and others — that identifies which thallenwort lineage, and by extension which extended kin-network, the individual was bonded into. Two Verdani sharing a kinstrain name are kin in the fullest sense Verdani law recognizes, whether or not they have ever met.

## Regional Variation

Kinbed coloration runs from the deep chlorophyll green of high-canopy strains adapted to filtered forest light, through the duller olive-brown of fenland strains adapted to reduced light beneath marsh mist, to the pale, high-reflectance strains of high-terrace populations adapted to intense unfiltered highland sun. Steppe strains run reddish, an adaptation Verdance's own botanists attribute to the accessory pigments steppe thallenwort produces to handle the biome's wide daily temperature swings. A Verdani's coloration is consequently a reasonably reliable indicator of kinstrain and, by extension, home biome — though Verdani caution outsiders, correctly, against treating it as a reliable indicator of anything about the individual beyond that.

## Interspecies Notes

Verdani cannot interbreed with humans or any other known species; thallenwort symbiosis itself cannot be transplanted into a non-Verdani host, despite recurring Federation medical interest in whether its metabolic benefits could be adapted for other physiologies. Verdani are otherwise fully compatible with standard humanoid life-support and atmospheric standards, unlike [Meridian](/star-rangers/lore/planets/meridian/)'s native population, and require only reliable access to full-spectrum light on any extended off-world posting — a standard accommodation most Federation and Star Ranger vessels already carry for unrelated reasons.
