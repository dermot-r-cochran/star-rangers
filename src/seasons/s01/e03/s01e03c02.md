:::pov galahad

He had meant to come back sooner.

That was not an excuse. Galahad did not deal in excuses; he dealt in the gap between what should have happened and what had, and he had learned to read that gap the way he read incident reports — for what it implied, not what it claimed. What it implied here was straightforward. He had spent the day at Threshold Station confirming a coupling between a chronometer drift and a stand of seven trees, and he had let the confirming feel like progress, when the actual unfinished work was three years old and standing in front of him three years ago in the person of a woman he had told, in careful survey language, less than he knew.

He had not lied to Elvira. He had simply done what twelve years in the Corps had taught him to do without noticing he was doing it: filed the sayable version and kept the rest for people cleared to read it.

She was not cleared. She had never needed to be. That had been the error.

He walked out to the causeway with the full survey archive on a single data slate — eleven years of chronometer discrepancy, the six prior postings, the two unexplained events he had never been asked to elaborate on, and Syra's model from that morning, the one that said *your safety margins become fiction* in front of the whole operations room. He had not trimmed any of it for tone. He did not know how Elvira would take being handed eleven years of someone else's careful omissions at once. He had decided that was not his decision to make for her.

She was waiting at the edge of the causeway stones, where she usually was at this hour, doing the slow work of resetting a circle that would fail again if nothing changed.

"I have something for you," he said. "All of it. Not the summary."

She looked at the slate, then at him, and did not reach for it yet.

"Why now?"

"Because I found out today that your failed circle and my station's clock are the same problem wearing two names," he said. "And because Aldera told me, three days ago, in a report I only half-understood at the time, that this is what breaks it. Not the ritual. The disclosure."

He held it out again.

"I should have done this the first evening," he said. "I filed it instead. That was a mistake, and it was mine, not the Corps'."

:::pov elvira

She read for eleven minutes without speaking.

Galahad let her. He did not fill the silence with the kind of reassurance that would have made the moment about his discomfort rather than her information, and she noted this and revised her model of him upward again, in the small, exact way she revised things that had already earned a high rating.

The archive was worse than she had expected and more useful than anything she had been given in three years. Six stations. Two unexplained. A drift that had been quietly incident-coded for over a decade because "boundary instrument fault" was a phrase that closed files instead of opening questions. A model, current as of this morning, connecting her causeway to a station a kilometre and a half away by a ratio that meant her failures had never been local at all.

She understood, reading it, precisely how much she had not known, and — more usefully — precisely what shape the not-knowing had taken. Ambiguity was not an absence. It was a specific, textured thing, and now that she could see its edges she could see where it had been sitting.

Not in the chalk. Not in the thread.

In the question she had never asked, because she had never thought to ask it of something that could not, by any classification she used, be asked anything at all.

She turned toward the reeds, where a particular quality of stillness had been present, at a slight distance, every single night for three years without her once addressing it directly.

"Aldera."

The stillness did not move immediately. When it did, it was unhurried — a cat choosing to be seen rather than being caught at it.

"You have been here longer than I have," Elvira said. "You know what this is. Not the method. I have that now, finally, from him." A short breath. "I am asking you to tell me the rest. All of it. What is feeding, and on what, precisely, and what stops it."

She had never said the sentence *I am asking you* to Aldera before. She had spoken near her, around her, past her, in the accumulated habit of three years of treating a watching cat as company rather than as an instrument. It cost her something small and specific to say it plainly. She said it plainly anyway, because plainness was the entire point.

A cat who announces a predator is a cat performing instinct. A cat who answers a direct question is a source.

The mechanism, in full, now that it has been asked for:

The entity is not feeding on the boundary drift. It is feeding on the *unresolved status of the failures* — the fact that each attempt ends without either subject knowing why, which the entity has been actively sustaining by shifting the pattern substrate a small, deniable amount between each attempt. Subject A's own unresolved classification — what she is, a question she has lived inside for longer than three years — supplied ambient ambiguity the entity used as cover. It did not need to feed on that question directly. It only needed the question to stay open near its hunting ground, the way a bank of fog does not need to be the predator to be useful to one.

I want this recorded precisely, because precision is the counter: naming Subject A's own nature is not required to end this. That question remains open. It is hers, not mine to close, and not this entity's either. What ends the feeding is narrower and achievable tonight — the *why* behind each failed circle, now fully known between the three of us, with no gap left for the entity to occupy.

Recommendation, filed and actioned as of this entry: nothing further from Subject A is required to break the current loop. The clarity itself is the intervention. The causeway's texture — a wrongness I have logged nightly for three years — is already changing as I file this. Reeds settling. Air pressure normalizing by approximately four percent over the last ninety seconds.

The root-anchor at the dryadic grove is a separate structural matter and remains unresolved; Rook-7's coupling model from this morning still applies, and that work is not tonight's.

Tonight's work is finished.

I was asked. I answered. Filing complete.
