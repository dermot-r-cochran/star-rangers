  <h1 class="lore-entry__title">UL Standards International Formed</h1>
  <p><span class="character-badge">Pre-USC era, ~Phase A/B transition</span></p>
</header>
<div class="lore-entry__body">

The global safety certification body whose lineage traces to Underwriters Laboratories — founded in Chicago in 1894 to provide independent safety testing for electrical equipment — merges with European and Asian standards organisations to form **UL Standards International**. Its mandate extends to all human-occupied off-world environments.

The move is driven not by institutional ambition but by commercial necessity: the operators deploying hardware in orbital, cislunar, and lunar environments need certified safety assessments their insurers will accept. UL Standards International is the body whose testing methodology those insurers can audit.

The expansion into boundary-proximate environments — where adjacent Concordants produce divergent local physical constants that break standard certification frameworks — defines the organisation's character for the centuries that follow. Rather than applying existing frameworks to inadequate environments, UL Standards International builds new ones from first principles, producing the first iteration of the *Boundary-Proximate Operations Standards* that will eventually underpin Survey Corps field certification.

**Canon facts:**
- The core methodology — define what failure looks like, test for it, certify those that pass — carries unbroken from 1894 to 2826 UCSD.
- UL Standards International is the largest single organisation absorbed into the Solar System Safety Standards Authority when the USC consolidates certification bodies in ~2340 UCSD.
- The SSSA is absorbed into the Star Rangers at founding in 2723 UCSD and reconstituted as the Safety Corps.
- The institutional lineage from a Chicago product-testing laboratory to the Solar System's primary boundary-operations certification authority spans approximately seven centuries without a break in core methodology.
